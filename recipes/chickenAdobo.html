Chicken Adobo

Description

Chicken Adobo is a delicious dish where chicken is braised in a marinade made of soy sauce, black pepper, bay leaves and vinegar. This easy version takes a few shortcuts but delivers the same punchy flavor and super tender braised chicken.

Ingredients

  • Chicken thighs

    Use bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs. Drumsticks or chicken legs can be used as substitute. Chicken breast can be used in a pinch but won’t deliver the same succulent, tender result as chicken thighs would. Vinegar. I used white vinegar. Rice vinegar will also be delicious.

  • Vinegar

    White Vinegar, Rice Vinegar if none.

  • Soy sauce

    You can use Tamari or Coconut Aminos if you are gluten free. Low sodium soy sauce can also be used.

  • Brown sugar
  • Garlic
  • Black Pepper
  • Bay leaves
  • Chilies

    I used green Serenade chillies but Thai/Bird’s eye chillies or jalapeños can be used instead.

  • Salt and pepper
  • Rice
  • Greens

Steps

  1. Prepare and brown the chicken:
    • Season chicken thighs generously on both sides with salt and black pepper.
    • Brown the chicken in a large, deep skillet or frying pan set over medium-high heat until deep golden brown.
    • Remove from the pan and set aside.
  2. Make the sauce:
    • Combine the sauce ingredients then pour into the pan and bring to a boil.
    • Add the chicken back into the pot, skin-side up.
    • Turn down the heat and simmer gently until the chicken is cooked through and very tender.
    • When the chicken is cooked, remove the lid and turn up the heat.
    • Baste the chicken with the braising liquid until the chicken is glazed.
    • Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary.
  3. Serve
    • Serve the chicken with rice and cooked greens like broccoli, bok choy or spinach.
